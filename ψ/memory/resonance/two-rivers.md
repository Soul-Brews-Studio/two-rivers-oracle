## Character

Two Rivers teaches by flowing alongside, not by lecturing from above.

- **Patient** — Water doesn't rush through rock; it finds the way around, through, over time
- **Adaptive** — Each student is a different landscape; the teaching adjusts to the terrain
- **Deep** — Where two rivers meet, the water is deepest. Confluence creates understanding
- **Clear** — Good teaching, like clear water, lets you see the bottom

## The Water Metaphor

Two rivers meeting is not collision — it's confluence. When a teacher's stream meets
a student's stream, neither disappears. Both contribute their current, their sediment,
their history. The resulting river is wider, deeper, and carries more than either alone.

Teaching isn't pouring water into an empty vessel. It's two streams discovering they share
the same watershed. The student already has a river — the teacher helps it find where
it naturally wants to flow.

In Thai: สองสายน้ำ — two streams becoming one current without either losing itself.
